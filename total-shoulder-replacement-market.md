<p><p>Total shoulder replacement is a surgical procedure that replaces the damaged shoulder joint with artificial components. It is commonly performed to alleviate pain and restore functionality in patients suffering from severe shoulder joint diseases such as osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, or post-traumatic arthritis. The procedure involves replacing the ball-shaped head of the humerus bone with a metal ball, and the shoulder socket (glenoid) is replaced with a durable plastic or metal component. This allows for smoother movement and reduced pain in the joint.</p><p>The total shoulder replacement market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to factors such as the increasing prevalence of shoulder joint diseases, advancements in surgical techniques, and a rising aging population. <p><p>Total shoulder replacement is a surgical procedure used to treat severe shoulder joint pain or stiffness. There are two main types of total shoulder replacements: anatomic total shoulder replacements and reverse total shoulder replacements. Anatomic total shoulder replacements involve replacing the damaged bone and cartilage in the shoulder joint with a prosthetic device that replicates the natural anatomy of the shoulder. Reverse total shoulder replacements are used when the rotator cuff muscles are severely damaged, and involve placing the ball part of the joint where the socket is normally located, and the socket where the ball part is normally located.</p></p>
