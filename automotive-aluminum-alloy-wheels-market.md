<p><p>Automotive aluminum alloy wheels are available in three main types: casting, forging, and other. Casting wheels are made by pouring molten aluminum into a mold, resulting in a cost-effective and versatile option. Forging wheels are created by shaping aluminum under high pressure, making them stronger and lighter than casting wheels. Other market types may include hybrid wheels combining casting and forging techniques, or specialized custom designs. Each type offers unique benefits in terms of strength, weight, and customization options for automotive applications.</p></p>
