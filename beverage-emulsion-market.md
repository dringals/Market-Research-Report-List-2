<p><p>Beverage emulsion refers to a mixture of two immiscible liquids, such as oil and water, stabilized with the help of an emulsifying agent. This emulsifying agent prevents separation of the two liquids and creates a stable and homogenous emulsion. Beverage emulsions are used in a variety of drinks, including alcoholic beverages, non-alcoholic beverages, dairy-based drinks, and fruit juices, among others, to enhance flavor, texture, and overall sensory experience.</p><p>The beverage emulsion market has witnessed significant growth in recent years. The primary factors driving this growth include the increasing demand for innovative and flavored beverages, growing consumer preferences for natural and clean-label ingredients, and rising consumption of ready-to-drink beverages. Beverage emulsions offer improved mouthfeel, stability, and shelf-life to the beverages, thereby enhancing their overall appeal.</p><p>Furthermore, the rising disposable income levels, changing lifestyles, and the influence of social media on food and beverage trends are also contributing to the market growth.
