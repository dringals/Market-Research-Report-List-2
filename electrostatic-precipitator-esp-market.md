<p><p>An Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) is a device used to remove particles from exhaust gas in industries. The market for ESPs is divided into three types:</p><p>1. Wet type: This type uses water to clean and capture the particles. The exhaust gas is passed through a water curtain, which collects the particles through impaction. Wet ESPs are suitable for sticky and corrosive particles.</p><p>2. Dry type: This type uses an electrostatic field to charge the particles, attracting them to collecting plates. The charged particles are then removed through mechanical rapping. Dry ESPs are preferred for dry, non-sticky particles.</p><p>3. Others: This market category includes hybrid ESPs, which combine wet and dry methods, as well as advanced technologies that use alternative energy sources or unique particle collection mechanisms. These options cater to specific industrial requirements or environmental regulations.</p></p>
