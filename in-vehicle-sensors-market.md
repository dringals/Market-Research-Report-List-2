LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) sensors use laser pulses to create detailed 3D maps of the surroundings and are vital for the perception and navigation systems of autonomous vehicles.</p><p>Moreover, the demand for advanced sensor technologies such as radar sensors, ultrasonic sensors, and image sensors is increasing to enable features like adaptive cruise control, collision avoidance, and blind-spot detection. <p><ul><li>Wired</li><li>Wireless</li></ul></p>
<p><p>In vehicle sensors market types refer to two main categories: wired and wireless. Wired sensors are physically connected to the vehicle's electronic system using cables or wires, allowing for continuous and reliable data transmission. On the other hand, wireless sensors use radio frequency or Bluetooth technology to transmit data without the need for physical connections. While wired sensors offer more stability and security, wireless sensors enable greater flexibility and easier installation. Both types play a crucial role in collecting and transmitting important vehicle data for various applications and functionalities.</p></p>
