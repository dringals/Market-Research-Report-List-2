<p><p>The Power Management Unit (PMU) market can be categorized into two types: centralized and distributed markets. In a centralized market, the power management functions are consolidated and controlled by a single entity or system. This allows for a more streamlined and coordinated approach to managing power distribution and utilization. On the other hand, in a distributed market, power management functions are spread across multiple entities or systems, allowing for more localized control and flexibility. This type of market often involves the integration of renewable energy sources and enables greater resilience and responsiveness to changes in power demand.</p></p>
