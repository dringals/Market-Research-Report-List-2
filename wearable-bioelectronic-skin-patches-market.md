<p><p>Wearable bioelectronic skin patches are devices that can be attached to the body to monitor various biological signals. There are different types of sensors used in these patches. Electrophysiological sensors measure electrical signals generated by the body, like heart rate or brain activity. Biochemical sensors detect specific substances in the body, like glucose levels or alcohol. Potentiometric sensors measure ion concentration, commonly used in sweat analysis. Tactile sensors provide information about physical contact and pressure applied to the skin. Overall, these sensors enable accurate and continuous monitoring of important health parameters.</p></p>
