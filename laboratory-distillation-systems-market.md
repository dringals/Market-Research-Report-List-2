<p><p>The laboratory distillation systems market includes various types such as distillation, steam distillation, vacuum distillation, and fractional distillation. Distillation involves heating a liquid mixture to separate the components based on different boiling points. Steam distillation is a technique specifically used for separating heat-sensitive compounds from plant materials. Vacuum distillation is carried out under reduced pressure to lower the boiling points of substances. Fractional distillation separates liquid mixtures into components with similar boiling points by using a fractionating column. These techniques are commonly used in laboratory settings for purification and separation purposes.</p></p>
