<p><p>The molecular spectroscopy instruments market consists of four major types: Near-Infrared Spectroscopy (NIRS), Fourier-Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR), Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR), and Ultraviolet-Visible Spectroscopy (UV-Vis). NIRS analyzes the interaction of light with molecules in the near-infrared region, while FTIR studies the absorption, emission, and scattering of infrared light. NMR utilizes the magnetic properties of atomic nuclei to determine molecular structure and composition. UV-Vis spectroscopy measures the absorption of ultraviolet and visible light to analyze chemical compounds. These instruments are widely used in various industries including pharmaceutical, biotechnology, and environmental monitoring for research and analysis purposes.</p></p>
