<p><p>Fractional flow reserve (FFR) measurement is a diagnostic technique that is used to assess the functional significance of coronary blockages in the heart. It involves measuring the pressure difference across a narrowing in the coronary arteries during maximum blood flow. FFR measurement is usually performed during coronary angiography and helps in determining whether a stenosis is hemodynamically significant and requires invasive treatment, such as angioplasty or stenting.</p><p>The fractional flow reserve measurement market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, the rising adoption of minimally invasive procedures, and advancements in FFR measurement technologies are some of the major factors driving market growth. <p><p>The Fractional Flow Reserve Measurement market comprises two main types: Guidewires and Monitoring Systems. Guidewires are thin wires inserted into the patient's blood vessels to measure blood flow and pressure. They provide real-time data and enable doctors to make informed decisions during cardiac procedures. On the other hand, Monitoring Systems are advanced devices that continuously monitor blood flow and provide accurate measurements to help diagnose and treat cardiovascular diseases. Both these types play a crucial role in improving patient outcomes by facilitating accurate assessment and treatment decisions.</p></p>
