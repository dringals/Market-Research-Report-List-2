<p><p>The Transmission Electron Microscope (TEM) market encompasses three types of microscopes: Conventional TEM, Low-Voltage Electron Microscope, and Cryo-microscopy. Conventional TEMs use an electron beam to illuminate and scan thin samples, offering high-resolution imaging. Low-Voltage Electron Microscopes operate at lower accelerating voltages, enhancing contrast, reducing sample damage, and allowing imaging of sensitive materials. Cryo-microscopy involves freezing the sample, enabling imaging of biological molecules and complexes in their native state. These different types cater to specific research needs, offering various advantages in terms of resolution, contrast, and sample preservation.</p></p>
