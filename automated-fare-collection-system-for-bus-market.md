A Single Journey Ticket allows passengers to purchase a one-time ticket for a specific journey, typically at a higher price. On the other hand, a Stored Value Ticket enables passengers to preload a certain amount of money onto a card or device, which is deducted each time they take a journey. These options provide flexibility and convenience for passengers while streamlining the fare collection process for bus operators.</p></p>
