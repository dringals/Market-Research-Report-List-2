<p><p>Crystalline fructose is a type of sweetener derived from fruits and vegetables. In the food market, there are two main types of crystalline fructose production processes: Starch Hydrolysis and Sucrose Hydrolysis. Starch Hydrolysis involves breaking down starch into glucose and then further converting it into fructose. On the other hand, Sucrose Hydrolysis involves breaking down sucrose into glucose and fructose. Both processes result in the production of crystalline fructose that is used as a sweetener in various food products.</p></p>
