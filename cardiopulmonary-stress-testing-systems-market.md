<p><p>Cardiopulmonary stress testing systems are used to assess the cardiovascular and pulmonary systems during exercise. Cardiopulmonary exercise testing (CPET) systems measure various parameters like oxygen consumption and heart rate to evaluate cardiopulmonary function. Stress ECG is a type of test that monitors the electrical activity of the heart during exercise. Pulse oximeters measure oxygen saturation levels in the blood, while stress blood pressure monitors track blood pressure changes during exercise. Single-Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) is a nuclear imaging technique that provides detailed images of the heart's blood flow during exercise.</p></p>
