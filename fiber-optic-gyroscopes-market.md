<p><p>Fiber Optic Gyroscopes (FOGs) are sensing devices that measure changes in orientation using the principles of light interference. They utilize the concept of the Sagnac effect, where a beam of light travels in opposite directions through a coiled fiber optic cable, resulting in a phase shift that correlates with the angular velocity of the gyroscope. FOGs are extensively used in various applications, including aerospace, defense, industrial, and automotive sectors, for accurate navigation, stabilization, and guidance purposes.</p><p>The future outlook for the Fiber Optic Gyroscopes Market appears highly promising. A 2-Axis FOG measures angular velocity around two orthogonal axes, useful for navigation and autonomous vehicle control. Other FOG variants may include custom configurations tailored to specific industry needs, offering additional flexibility and functionality.</p></p>
