One of the key trends is the transition from traditional switches to fabric-based switches, driven by the need for higher bandwidth and low-latency networks. The demand for fabric-based switches is expected to increase as they offer improved scalability, flexibility, and performance.</p><p>Another trend is the adoption of software-defined networking (SDN) and network function virtualization (NFV) technologies. SDN allows the separation of network control and data plane, enhancing network programmability, while NFV enables the virtualization of network functions, reducing the need for dedicated hardware. The integration of switch fabric with SDN and NFV technologies is expected to drive market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing deployment of Ethernet switches in data centers and the increasing demand for high-speed interconnects to support cloud services are propelling the switch fabric market.
