<p><p>The stainless steel ingots market can be categorized into two types: hot strapping and cold strapping. Hot strapping refers to a process where stainless steel ingots are bundled together while they are still being heated. This method is commonly used to facilitate transportation and storage. On the other hand, cold strapping involves the binding of already cooled stainless steel ingots. This technique is often used when stainless steel ingots need to be securely packaged for shipment or storage. Both hot and cold strapping methods play a crucial role in the stainless steel ingots market for efficient handling and logistics purposes.</p></p>
