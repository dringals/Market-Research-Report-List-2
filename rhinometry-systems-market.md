<p><p>Rhinometry systems are used to evaluate and diagnose nasal conditions. Rhinomanometry systems measure nasal airway resistance and pressure, helping to assess nasal congestion. Rhinoresistometry systems also measure airway resistance but focus on specific areas within the nasal passages. Acoustic rhinometry systems use sound waves to create a visual image of the nose and its dimensions, providing information on nasal swelling and obstruction. These different types of rhinometry systems offer various approaches to assess and understand nasal function for medical professionals.</p></p>
