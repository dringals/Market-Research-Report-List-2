<p><p>Microsurgery Electrosurgical Units (ESUs) are medical devices used in minimally invasive surgical procedures that require precise cutting and coagulation of tissues. There are three main types of ESUs: Monopole ESUs, which use a single active electrode for cutting and coagulation; Bipolar ESUs, which use two active electrodes for safer and more controlled tissue manipulation; and Vessel Sealing Units, which use advanced technology to seal blood vessels during surgery. Each type of ESU has its own unique benefits and applications in microsurgery.</p></p>
