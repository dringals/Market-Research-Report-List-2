<p><p>Overactive bladder (OAB) is a condition characterized by sudden and frequent urges to urinate, often resulting in involuntary leakage. Diagnosis of OAB involves a comprehensive evaluation of medical history, physical examination, and specialized tests such as urodynamic studies and bladder diary. These tests help to rule out other underlying conditions and determine the severity of the symptoms.</p><p>Treatment options for OAB include lifestyle changes, medication, behavior therapy, and in severe cases, surgery. Lifestyle changes may include limiting fluid intake and avoiding bladder irritants such as caffeine and alcohol. Medications commonly prescribed for OAB include anticholinergics and beta-3 adrenergic agonists. Behavior therapy includes bladder training, pelvic floor exercises, and biofeedback techniques. In certain cases, surgical interventions like sacral nerve stimulation or the injection of botulinum toxin into the bladder may be considered.</p><p>In recent years, the overactive bladder diagnosis and treatment market has witnessed significant growth. The increasing prevalence of OAB, growing awareness among patients, and advancements in diagnostic technologies are driving market growth.
