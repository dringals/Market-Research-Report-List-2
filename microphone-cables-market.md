<p><ul><li>Balanced (Two-core Outsourcing Shielding)</li><li>Unbalanced (Single Core Outsourcing Shield)</li></ul></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Microphone cables can be categorized into two market types: balanced and unbalanced. Balanced microphone cables typically have two cores, each carrying an audio signal in opposite polarities. These cables are commonly used in professional audio setups as they effectively minimize interference and noise, ensuring a high-quality audio transmission. On the other hand, unbalanced microphone cables have a single core carrying the audio signal and are more susceptible to interference and noise. They are often used in consumer-oriented applications or shorter cable runs where the audio quality requirement is less demanding.</p></p>
