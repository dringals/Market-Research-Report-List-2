<p><p>Lazy eye, also known as amblyopia, refers to a vision development disorder that typically occurs in childhood. There are three main types of lazy eye market: strabismic, refractive amblyopia, and deprivation amblyopia. Strabismic amblyopia is caused by misalignment of the eyes, resulting in decreased vision in the affected eye. Refractive amblyopia is caused by a difference in refractive error between the eyes. Deprivation amblyopia is caused by a physical obstruction or deprivation of visual input in one eye. These market types contribute to the overall prevalence of lazy eye among children and require early detection and intervention for optimal treatment.</p></p>
